Bibingkoy

Bibingkoy is a Filipino rice cake originating from Cavite City. It consists of baked glutinous rice dough filled with sweetened mung beans and served with tapioca and jackfruit in a sweetened coconut milk sauce.

prep time

30 mins

cook time

35 mins

serving

1 serving

instructions

step 1

Place glutinous rice flour in a bowl and gradually add water while mixing with your hands. Continue incorporating water until it forms a dough. Knead for 5 minutes.

step 2

Take a small piece of the dough, flatten it, and place a tablespoon of sweetened red bean paste in the center. Seal it to form a ball slightly smaller than a golf ball. Arrange the balls in a greased banana leaf-lined baking pan.

step 3

Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C for 10 minutes. Then switch the oven to the broil or grill setting at 180°C and continue for another 10 minutes. Remove from the oven.

step 4

For the topping, in a large saucepan, combine coconut milk, jackfruit, and sugar. Bring it to a boil, then simmer over low heat for 10 minutes or until the top is golden brown.

step 5

Add the cooked tapioca and continue to cook for an additional 3 minutes. Turn off the heat.

step 6

To serve, pour the cooked sauce on top of the bibingkoy. Enjoy!

ingredients

4 cup glutinous flour

1 cup sweetened red bean paste

water

banana leaf

2 cup coconut milk

1/2 cup ripe jackfruit, cut into strips

1 cup white sugar

1/2 cup cooked tapioca pearls
