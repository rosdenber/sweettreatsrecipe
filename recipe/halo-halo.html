Halo-halo

Halo-halo is a popular and iconic Filipino dessert that translates to "mix-mix" in English. It is a colorful and refreshing concoction that combines a variety of ingredients to create a delightful medley of flavors and textures. Halo-halo is especially favored during the hot summer months in the Philippines, providing a cool and satisfying treat.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

0 mins

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Follow the package instructions to prepare the gulaman, using less water than recommended for a firmer texture.

step 2

Either prepare the ingredients from scratch or use pre-packaged halo-halo ingredients.

step 3

In a tall glass, add approximately a teaspoon of each desired add-in until the glass is about ⅓ full.

step 4

Fill the glass with shaved ice.

step 5

Drizzle evaporated milk over the ice.

step 6

Place leche flan and a scoop of ice cream on top of the ice.

step 7

Optionally, garnish with strips of macapuno and sprinkle with pinipig.

step 8

Serve immediately.

ingredients

½ cup gulaman cubes or sago

½ cup sweetened beans (garbanzo, red mung, and/or white beans)

½ cup Nata de coco, drained well

½ cup Kaong, drained well

½ cup sweetened saba bananas

½ cup sweetened jackfruit strips

¼ cup sweetened macapuno

½ cup ube halaya

shaved ice

1 cup evaporated milk

1 cup leche flan

4 scoops ice cream (mango, ube, or vanilla)
