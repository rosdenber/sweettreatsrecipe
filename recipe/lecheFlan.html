Leche Flan

Leche flan is a rich and creamy caramel custard dessert that has Spanish origins but has become an integral part of Filipino cuisine. The name "leche flan" is derived from the Spanish term "flan de leche," which translates to "milk flan." It is a popular dessert in the Philippines and is often served during special occasions, celebrations, and family gatherings.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

45 mins

serving

6 servings

instructions

step 1

Utilize all the eggs by separating the yolk from the egg white, using only the egg yolks.

step 2

Place the egg yolks in a large bowl and beat them thoroughly using a fork or an egg beater.

step 3

Add the condensed milk and mix it in thoroughly.

step 4

Pour in the fresh milk and vanilla, and mix the ingredients well.

step 5

Place the mold (llanera) on the stove and heat it over low fire.

step 6

Sprinkle granulated sugar on the mold, mixing it until the solid sugar transforms into liquid caramel with a light brown color. Note: Depending on your location, it may be challenging to find a traditional flan mold (llanera). Using individual round pans can be a more convenient alternative for making leche flan.

step 7

Evenly spread the caramel (liquid sugar) on the flat side of the mold.

step 8

Wait for 5 minutes, then pour the egg yolk and milk mixture onto the mold.

step 9

Cover the top of the mold with aluminum foil.

step 10

Steam the mold with the egg and milk mixture for 30 to 35 minutes.

step 11

After steaming, let the temperature cool down, then refrigerate.

step 12

Serve the dessert and share the enjoyment!

ingredients

10 pieces eggs

1 can condensed milk (14 oz)

1 cup fresh milk or evaporated milk

1 cup granulated sugar

1 teaspoon vanilla extract
