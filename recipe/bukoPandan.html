Buko Pandan

Buko pandan is a popular and refreshing Filipino dessert that highlights the flavors of young coconut (buko) and screwpine (pandan) leaves. It is a sweet and creamy dessert that is often served chilled, making it a delightful treat, especially during warm weather.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

10 mins

serving

4 servings

instructions

step 1

Mix water and powdered gelatin together and stir with a spoon.

step 2

Add Buko Pandan flavoring and stir until the mixture is evenly combined.

step 3

Heat a saucepan, pour in the mixture, and bring it to a boil while stirring continuously.

step 4

Turn off the heat, transfer the mixture to a mold, and let it cool. The texture should become firm upon cooling. For a quicker result, you can refrigerate it after allowing it to cool to room temperature.

step 5

Combine condensed milk, table cream, sago pearls, and young coconut, then mix thoroughly. Let the mixture thicken by chilling in the refrigerator or freezer for a few hours.

step 6

Slice the firm gelatin into 1-inch cubes and combine it with the condensed milk-cream-young coconut-sago mixture.

step 7

Transfer the mixture to individual serving platters or cups and top each with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

step 8

Serve for dessert. Share and enjoy!

ingredients

1 1/2 cup young coconut cut into strips

5 ounces condensed milk

8 ounces Table cream or all-purpose cream

3 ounces powdered gelatin

1 1/4 cups water

6 drops Buko Pandan flavoring

2 scoops vanilla ice cream optional

1/2 cup sago pearls cooked (optional)
