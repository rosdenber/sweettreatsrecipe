Ginataang Bilo-Bilo

Ginataang bilo-bilo is a delicious and comforting Filipino dessert that features glutinous rice balls (bilo-bilo) cooked in coconut milk (ginataan). It is a sweet and hearty dish with a variety of colorful ingredients, making it visually appealing as well. The name "ginataang" refers to the use of coconut milk in the dish.

prep time

15 mins

cook time

40 mins

serving

8 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat about 6 cups of water in a pot over medium heat until it reaches a boil. Add sago pearls and cook, stirring occasionally, for approximately 15 to 20 minutes or until they become soft and translucent. Rinse and drain thoroughly.

step 2

Combine glutinous rice flour and ½ cup water in a small bowl to form a pliable dough. Adjust the consistency by adding more flour if it's too sticky or a bit more water if it's too crumbly.

step 3

Take about a teaspoon of the dough and use the palms of your hands to shape it into smooth balls. Cover the prepared balls with a cloth until you're ready to use them.

step 4

In a pot over medium heat, mix coconut milk and sugar. Bring it to a simmer, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves. Continue cooking until it slightly reduces.

step 5

Add the glutinous rice balls and cook for around 8 to 10 minutes or until they start floating to the top.

step 6

Introduce coconut cream and stir to combine. Continue cooking until it slightly thickens.

step 7

Include sago and jackfruit, continuing to cook for about 3 to 5 minutes or until heated through.

step 8

Ladle the mixture into bowls and, if desired, top with additional coconut cream.

ingredients

water

½ cup uncooked mini sago pearls

1 cup glutinous rice flour

4 cups coconut milk

1 cup sugar

1 cup coconut cream

1 cup ripe jackfruit shredded
