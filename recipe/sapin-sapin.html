Sapin-Sapin

Sapin-sapin is a colorful and layered Filipino dessert that is not only delicious but also visually appealing. The name "sapin-sapin" translates to "layers" in English, which perfectly describes the distinctive appearance of this sweet treat. It is a popular kakanin (native delicacy) in Filipino cuisine, often enjoyed during special occasions, celebrations, and holidays.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

50 mins

serving

12 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat coconut cream in a pan over medium heat until it boils. Stir occasionally and cook until the liquid thickens.

step 2

Reduce heat, stir, and scrape the sides to prevent burning as oil separates and solids form. Continue cooking and stirring until curds turn golden brown.

step 3

Drain the latik from the oil and store it separately until ready to use.

step 4

Brush the inner sides and bottom of an 8-inch round baking pan generously with oil.

step 5

Combine glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, condensed milk, and sugar in a large bowl. Stir until sugar dissolves and the mixture is smooth.

step 6

Strain the batter with a fine-mesh sieve and divide it evenly into three bowls.

step 7

In one bowl, stir in ube extract until the color is well dispersed. In another bowl, add Langka extract and stir until the color is evenly distributed. The remaining portion remains white and plain.

step 8

Pour the ube-flavored batter into the prepared baking pan and steam for about 10 minutes until set.

step 9

Pour the langka-flavored batter over the purple layer and steam for another 10 minutes until set.

step 10

Gently pour the plain batter over the yellow layer and steam for about 10 minutes until set.

step 11

Remove the cake pan from the steamer and let it cool completely.

step 12

Run a knife around the sides of the pan to loosen the rice cake. Invert the pan onto a wide, flat serving platter, tapping it to release the sapin-sapin (purple layer now on top).

step 13

Brush the top and sides of the sapin-sapin with coconut oil, sprinkle latik on top, and cut the cake into serving portions.

ingredients

1 cup coconut cream (kakang gata)

3 Â½ cups glutinous rice flour

2 cans (13.5 ounces each) coconut milk

1 can (14 ounces) condensed milk

1 cup sugar

3 drops ube flavor extract

3 drops langka flavor extract
