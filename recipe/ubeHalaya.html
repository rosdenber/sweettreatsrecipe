Ube Halaya

Ube halaya, also known as purple yam jam, is a popular Filipino dessert that features ube, a vibrant purple yam. The word "halaya" in Filipino refers to a jam-like or pudding-like consistency. Ube halaya is made by grating or mashing boiled purple yams, which have a sweet and nutty flavor, and then cooking them with ingredients like condensed milk, coconut milk, butter, and sugar until a thick, smooth, and rich purple paste is formed.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

1 hr

serving

12 servings

instructions

step 1

Combine the grated ube, coconut milk, condensed milk, evaporated milk, butter, and sugar in a heavy-bottomed skillet over medium heat.

step 2

Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally, until the butter melts, the ube spreads, and the sugar dissolves.

step 3

If using, add the ube extract and stir until the mixture takes on an even color.

step 4

Reduce heat and simmer, stirring frequently, until a soft, sticky dough forms, 30 to 40 minutes.

step 5

Melt the cheese by stirring it in. Simmer for an additional 10 to 15 minutes, or until mixture thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon and pulls away slightly from the pan's edge.

step 6

Grease a baking dish or llanera mold with melted butter or coconut oil just enough to coat the sides.

step 7

Transfer ube halaya into the mold or dish that has been prepared, then spread and smooth it out evenly with a buttered spatula. Let it cool.

step 8

Turn the halaya over onto a serving platter to serve. Pour a thin layer of coconut oil over the surface and add sweetened macapuno, shredded cheese, latik, or toasted coconut flakes as garnish.
