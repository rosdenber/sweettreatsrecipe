Maja Blanca

Maja blanca is a traditional Filipino dessert that is well-loved for its creamy and delicate texture. The name "maja blanca" is derived from the Spanish word "blanca," meaning white, which reflects the dessert's pale color. This sweet treat is often served during special occasions, celebrations, and holidays in Filipino culture.

prep time

8 mins

cook time

35 mins

serving

8 servings

instructions

step 1

Heat coconut milk in a cooking pot until it reaches a boiling point.

step 2

Add sugar, condensed milk, and whole sweet kernel corn, stirring until all ingredients are evenly mixed.

step 3

Simmer the mixture for 8 minutes.

step 4

Mix milk and cornstarch, whisking until the cornstarch is fully dissolved.

step 5

Pour the fresh milk and cornstarch mixture into the cooking pot, stirring thoroughly.

step 6

Cook while stirring until the mixture achieves the desired thickness.

step 7

Transfer the mixture to a serving tray and flatten the top using a flat tool, such as a wooden spatula.

step 8

Allow it to cool down, then refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

step 9

Garnish with toasted grated coconut (or latik if available).

step 10

Serve the dessert cold and share the enjoyment!

ingredients

4 cups coconut milk

3/4 cup cornstarch

14 ounces condensed milk

3/4 cup fresh milk

3/4 cup granulated sugar

15 ounces whole sweet kernel corn

5 tbsp toasted grated coconut
