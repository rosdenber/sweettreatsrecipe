Baye Baye

Baye baye is a classic Filipino dessert that hails from the Iloilo region. It involves a mixture of grated young coconut and either corn patties or young rice pounded into flakes known as pinipig. To create baye baye, the pinipig is toasted until it achieves a golden brown color, then ground or pounded into flakes. These flakes are then mixed with coconut water, sugar, and grated young coconut to complete the dish.

prep time

10 mins

cook time

15 mins

serving

12 serving

instructions

step 1

On a heated skillet over low heat, toast the pinipig until it turns golden brown.

step 2

Grind the toasted pinipig using a blender or a mortar and pestle. In a bowl, combine the ground pinipig with coconut water and sugar.

step 3

Blend the mixture thoroughly and incorporate grated young coconuts.

step 4

Divide the mixture into serving portions. Brush coconut oil onto each banana leaf or wax paper wrapper.

step 5

Wrap each serving in banana leaves or wax paper, shaping them into log shapes.

step 6

Refrigerate before serving.

ingredients

2 cups pinipig

1 cup coconut water

1/2 cup sugar

1 1/2 cups butong young coconut, grated

coconut oil for brushing wrapper
