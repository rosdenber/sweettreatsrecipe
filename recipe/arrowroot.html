Arrowroot Cookies

Uraró, alternatively referred to as araró or arrowroot cookies, are a type of Filipino cookie crafted from arrowroot flour. These cookies boast a dry and powdery consistency, often taking on a flower-shaped form. Their roots trace back to the Tagalog community of southern Luzon, specifically in the provinces of Laguna, Quezon, and Marinduque.

prep time

25 mins

cook time

15 mins

serving

80 serving

instructions

step 1

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.

step 2

In a large bowl, sift together arrowroot flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt. Set the mixture aside.

step 3

In another mixing bowl, use an electric mixer to cream butter and sugar until it becomes creamy and fluffy.

step 4

Add the eggs one at a time, beating just until incorporated. Mix in the vanilla extract.

step 5

Gradually incorporate the dry ingredients into the mixing bowl, starting at a low speed to moisten and then increasing the speed to fully combine.

step 6

Beat the batter just until it becomes smooth. Avoid overbeating.

step 7

Transfer the batter into a cookie press. Dispense the batter in a single press onto an ungreased baking sheet, leaving about 1 and ½ inches of space between each.

step 8

Bake in the preheated 350-degree Fahrenheit oven for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the undersides start to turn golden.

step 9

Allow the cookies to cool completely in the pan. To remove them, simply slide a cookie with your fingers until it releases. Avoid using a spatula, as the cookies are fragile and may break.

ingredients

¾ cup butter, softened

¾ cup sugar

2 eggs

½ teaspoon vanilla extract

½ cup all-purpose flour

1 ½ teaspoons baking powder

2 cups arrowroot starch/flour

½ teaspoon salt
